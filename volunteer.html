what we do

The Tennessee Department of Agriculture, working with other agencies and non-governmental organization, is maintaining preparedness to respond to disasters. By coordinating the efforts of credentialed responders, as well as registered volunteers, the Disaster Animal Response Team (DART) is an integral part of animal disaster preparedness and response.

how to volunteer

Volunteering with DART is simple. To volunteer, first attend one of our monhtly meetings to discuss your interest in joining DART. Then we will provide you with the information to start your Registration or Credentialing process.

If training is what you're worried about, then don't be! All the training you need will be provided in special monthly meetings to that you can be a Credetialed Responder.

Registered volunteer vs Credentialed Responder

The Registered Volunteer can help with transporting supplies, cleaning the shelter/cages, etc. but is not supposed to have hands on with the animals. The requirement is the State Credentialing Class.

The Credentialed Responder can do anything including hands on with the animals. The requirements are the State Credentialing Class plus ICS 100, ICS 200, ICS (NIMS) 700 and 800 on the FEMA.gov website. (Links found below.)

Completing FEMAs Independent Study Courses, linked below, will prepare you for Tennessee State DART Credentialing Class, offered by the Tennessee Department of Agriculture, State Veterinarianâ€™s Office.

  • FEMA IS-100. Introduction to ICS, Incident Command System
  • FEMA IS- 200 Incident Command System for Single Resources/ Initial Action
  • FEMA IS -700 An Intro to National Incident Management System (NIMS)
  • FEMA IS -800 National Response Framework Intro
  • FEMA IS -10.A: Animals in Disasters: Awareness and Preparedness
  • FEMA IS -11.A Animals in Disasters: Community Planning
age limit

Teens may assist with the shelter functions as long as a parent or guardian is in attendance and performing their duties. The requirement is the State Credentialing Class, upon which they will be Registered or Credentialed upon turning 18.

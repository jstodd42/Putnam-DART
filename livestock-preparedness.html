Livestock Preparedness

Preparing for disasters and making prep kits

The priorities for disaster planning for farms and horse owners vary to some extent with the type of animals and facility. However, you should assemble a disaster kit that includes:

  • Current list of animals, including their location and feeding records, vaccinations, and tests. Make sure you have proof of ownership for all animals.
  • Supplies for temporary animal identification, such as plastic neckbands and permanent markers to label your animals.
  • Handling equipment such as halters, cages, and appropriate tools for each kind of animal.
  • Water, feed, and buckets to help feed and water your animals.
  • Tools and supplies needed for sanitation.
  • Safety and emergency items for your vehicles and trailers.
  • Have a plan for evacuating your animals. This includes making arrangments to have your animals moved and transported if you do not have the ability to do so.

Check the contents regularly to ensure fresh and complete supplies. Additional resources can be found at FEMA and the USDA.

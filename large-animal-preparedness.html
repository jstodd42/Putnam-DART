Disaster Preparedness for Large Animals including Horses

How to prepare for disasters and plan for the safety of your large animals

A good disaster plan is vital to keeping yourself and your animal companions safe during disaster situations. Since there may not be much time to prepare during an emergency, take the time now to create an effective plan.

  • Permanently identify each horse, cow, and other large animal by tatoo, chip, or photograph. In your records, include the animals age, gender, breed, color, and any other specific markings or characteristics. Keep this information in your important records.
  • Keep halters ready for your horses and other large animals.
  • Make arrangments in advance to have your horsea and other large animals trailered in case of an emergency. Make sure to have people on standby to help evacuate.
  • Large animals can sometimes be uncomfortable being loaded into trailers. Be sure to practice this procedure before disaster strikes.
  • Know where you can take your animals in an emergency evacuation situation.
  • If you CANNOT evacuate your animals, have a backup plan in case it is impossible. Consider different types of senarios and make a plan for each if you cannot evacuate. Check with your local emergency management team, your local DART team, agricultural extension agent, or local emergency management agency for information about your community's disaster response plans.
  • When disaster strikes, don't leave your animal behind. If a situation isn't safe for you, it will not be safe for your animals either.
  • Evacuate immediately. If you wait, there might be limited options for your animals. This could mean that your animals are left without food, water, or care for days.
  • SUPPLIES: Prepare basic first aid kit that is portable and easy to access. Include 12-20 gallons per day for animal,hay, feed, and medications for several days if needed.
